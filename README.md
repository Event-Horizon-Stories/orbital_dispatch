# orbital_dispatch

`orbital_dispatch` follows the moment an interplanetary fleet learns that
hearing about a problem is not the same thing as carrying the work through.

A relay can tumble half a degree off plane and lose line of sight on the next
conjunction. A patrol skiff can miss its insertion burn and drift into a wider
recovery arc. A docking collar can crack under thermal cycling and stay
"acknowledged" for three shifts while the station continues bleeding reaction
mass into vacuum. The signals arrive. The intent exists. The work still dies in
the gap between now and later.

That is the pressure this series is built around. Oban appears here as the
natural shape of a world where obligations must survive restart, delay, failure,
radiation blackout, and human absence.

This repo is still under construction. The chapter plan lives in
[`PLAN.md`](./PLAN.md).

## Planned Journey

The first planned arc follows the hardening of one dispatch office from ad hoc
follow-up into durable interplanetary obligation:

1. `01_missed_burn_window`
   A repair order exists only in memory and vanishes after a restart.
2. `02_retry_in_radiation`
   A launch fails in a charged-particle storm and must retry with backoff.
3. `03_scheduled_transfer`
   A replacement part is ready now, but the receiving station can only accept
   approach during a later transfer window.
4. `04_patrol_orbit`
   Recurring patrol and inspection work becomes durable instead of tribal
   intention.
5. `05_duplicate_distress`
   The same distress call reaches the office twice, and duplicate response
   becomes its own operational hazard.
6. `06_priority_corridor`
   Life-support failures and routine inspections collide under load, forcing the
   queue to reveal what matters first.
7. `07_verification_pass`
   Finishing one job creates another: repair launches are followed by inspection
   flybys and corridor-clearance checks.
8. `08_exhausted_escalation`
   The queue becomes answerable when retries still fail and the office has to
   surface exhausted work honestly.
